# Playbook: Procurement / Vendor Onboarding

Internal reference only — grounding for `workflow-def`'s best-practice suggestions. Never shown verbatim to the interviewee; use it to recognize which canonical step a described action maps to, and to notice gaps.

## Canonical steps

1. Need identified — a team requests a new tool/vendor/service.
2. Business justification and budget owner identified.
3. Vendor options evaluated (at least a lightweight comparison, not single-source by default).
4. Security/privacy review (data handled, access needed, compliance posture).
5. Legal review of contract terms (MSA/DPA/SLA).
6. Budget approval from the appropriate authority level.
7. Contract signed and stored centrally (not in someone's inbox).
8. Vendor added to a central vendor/tool registry with owner and renewal date.
9. Access provisioned to the tool, scoped to the requesting team.
10. Renewal/cancellation reminder scheduled ahead of the contract end date.
11. Periodic vendor review (usage, cost, continued need).

## Commonly missed steps

- No central vendor registry — nobody has a full list of what's paid for and who owns it.
- No security/privacy review before a vendor gets access to company data.
- No renewal-date tracking — contracts auto-renew unnoticed.
- Contract stored in an individual's inbox instead of a shared, findable location.
- No periodic review to catch unused/duplicate tools.

## Needed documents

- Vendor request/intake form
- Vendor security & privacy risk-assessment questionnaire
- MSA / DPA / SLA contract templates
- Vendor registry/tracker template
- Budget approval request form
- Renewal/cancellation checklist
