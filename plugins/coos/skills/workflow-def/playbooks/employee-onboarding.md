# Playbook: Employee Onboarding

Internal reference only — grounding for `workflow-def`'s best-practice suggestions. Never shown verbatim to the interviewee; use it to recognize which canonical step a described action maps to, and to notice gaps.

## Canonical steps

1. Offer accepted & start date confirmed — triggers onboarding.
2. Contract / employment paperwork generated and signed.
3. Accounts provisioned (email, SSO, core SaaS tools) before day one.
4. Hardware ordered/prepped and shipped or ready at desk.
5. Access granted to role-specific systems (least-privilege, not blanket admin).
6. Manager assigns an onboarding buddy or point of contact.
7. Day-one orientation: company overview, policies, tools walkthrough.
8. Role-specific ramp plan with 30/60/90-day goals.
9. Benefits enrollment completed within the required window.
10. Introduction to team, stakeholders, and recurring meetings.
11. First 1:1 with manager scheduled within the first week.
12. Probation/check-in milestones scheduled (e.g. 30/60/90 day reviews).

## Commonly missed steps

- No documented access-revocation trigger tied to role change (creates offboarding debt later).
- Hardware/account provisioning not started until day one, causing a dead first day.
- No named buddy/point of contact distinct from the manager.
- Benefits enrollment deadline not tracked, risking a lapse.
- No 30/60/90-day check-in cadence defined — onboarding stops being "owned" after week one.

## Needed documents

- Offer letter / employment contract template
- Onboarding checklist (day-1 through 90-day)
- IT equipment / access request form
- Employee handbook (policy acknowledgment)
- Benefits enrollment form
- NDA / confidentiality agreement
- Tax withholding & emergency contact forms
- Role-specific ramp plan template
