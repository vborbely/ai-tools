# Playbook: Sales Lead-to-Cash

Internal reference only — grounding for `workflow-def`'s best-practice suggestions. Never shown verbatim to the interviewee; use it to recognize which canonical step a described action maps to, and to notice gaps.

## Canonical steps

1. Lead captured (inbound form, outbound prospecting, referral) with source tracked.
2. Lead qualified against defined ICP/criteria (e.g. BANT/MEDDIC-style check).
3. Discovery call/meeting booked and held.
4. Needs and requirements documented.
5. Proposal/quote prepared and sent.
6. Objection handling and negotiation.
7. Contract/order form sent for signature.
8. Deal closed-won (or closed-lost with a logged reason).
9. Handoff to onboarding/customer success with context transferred (not just a CRM field flip).
10. Invoice generated and sent per agreed terms.
11. Payment collected and reconciled.
12. Deal and revenue recorded for reporting/forecasting.

## Commonly missed steps

- No qualification step — every lead gets a full sales cycle regardless of fit.
- Closed-lost reasons not captured, losing a feedback loop for messaging/product.
- Handoff to onboarding is a status change with no context transfer, forcing the customer to re-explain themselves.
- No formal reconciliation between signed contract terms and what's actually invoiced.
- Lead source not tracked, making channel ROI impossible to measure.

## Needed documents

- Lead qualification checklist (ICP criteria)
- Discovery call template/question guide
- Proposal/quote template
- Contract/order form template
- Sales-to-CS handoff document template
- Invoice template
- Closed-lost reason taxonomy
