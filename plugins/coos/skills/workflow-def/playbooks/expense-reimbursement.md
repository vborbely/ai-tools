# Playbook: Expense Reimbursement

Internal reference only — grounding for `workflow-def`'s best-practice suggestions. Never shown verbatim to the interviewee; use it to recognize which canonical step a described action maps to, and to notice gaps.

## Canonical steps

1. Expense incurred — employee pays out of pocket or on a corp card.
2. Receipt/invoice captured at time of purchase.
3. Expense submitted through a defined tool/form with category and business justification.
4. Policy check against spend limits and eligible categories.
5. Manager approval (first-line).
6. Finance/AP review for policy compliance and coding (cost center, GL account).
7. Escalation path for out-of-policy or high-value expenses (secondary approval).
8. Reimbursement processed on a defined payment cycle.
9. Confirmation sent to the employee once paid.
10. Expense archived/reconciled against accounting records.

## Commonly missed steps

- No spend-limit or category policy enforced at submission time — everything relies on manual review.
- No defined escalation path for out-of-policy or unusually large expenses.
- Receipts not required/verified, creating audit risk.
- No SLA on reimbursement turnaround, leaving employees unsure when they'll be paid back.
- Corporate card spend and out-of-pocket reimbursement handled as two disconnected processes.

## Needed documents

- Expense policy document (spend limits, eligible categories)
- Expense report/claim form template
- Approval matrix (who approves what amount)
- Receipt retention policy
- Corporate card usage agreement
